My research sits between photonic hardware and computational methods. Integrated photonic systems can generate rich spectral responses, but interpreting and optimizing these responses often requires careful modeling, signal processing, and algorithmic design.

In photonic sensing, a device response is not only determined by geometry, but also by coupling, loss, resonance conditions, environmental refractive index, and measurement strategy. This makes the design problem naturally multi-parameter and often multi-objective.

I am interested in computational photonics because it provides a way to connect physical intuition with algorithmic search. Simulation, optimization, and machine learning can help us understand how device parameters influence spectra and how measured signals can be reconstructed or interpreted.

This blog will collect my research notes on integrated photonics, microwave photonic signal processing, optimization, and machine-learning-assisted photonic design.
