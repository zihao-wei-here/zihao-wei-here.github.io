My research is centered on computational and integrated photonic systems, with a focus on photonic sensing, microwave photonic signal processing, and AI-assisted design.

## Research Themes

### Integrated Photonic Sensing

I study silicon photonic sensing structures such as microring resonators and subwavelength grating waveguides. My current work focuses on localized subwavelength grating ring resonators, where the sensing region is designed around the coupling region to study wavelength-dependent coupling, resonance shifts, extinction ratio, insertion loss, and sensitivity.

### Microwave Photonic Signal Processing

I have worked on microwave photonic filter design for high-resolution spectrum analysis. This includes generating filter responses, evaluating autocorrelation and cross-correlation properties, and exploring reconstruction algorithms based on compressed measurements.

### Optimization and AI-assisted Design

Many photonic design problems involve high-dimensional, nonlinear, and multi-objective parameter spaces. I am interested in using computational search, Bayesian optimization, evolutionary algorithms, and machine learning methods to assist photonic structure design and signal reconstruction.

## Selected Projects

### Localized Subwavelength Grating Ring Resonator for Photonic Sensing

This project studies a partially covered silicon microring resonator with a localized subwavelength grating region near the coupling section. The goal is to analyze how structural parameters affect resonance behavior, sensing performance, extinction ratio, and insertion loss.

Methods used:
- Lumerical 3D-FDTD for localized coupling-region simulation
- MODE for waveguide and propagation-region analysis
- INTERCONNECT for circuit-level spectral modeling
- MATLAB/Python for spectral post-processing and metric extraction

### High-resolution Microwave Photonic Filter Design

This project explores the design of multi-channel microwave photonic filters for spectrum analysis and reconstruction. The work includes parameterized filter generation, correlation-based evaluation, and algorithmic reconstruction from compressed power readings.

Methods used:
- Python and MATLAB simulation
- Autocorrelation and cross-correlation analysis
- Parameter search and multi-objective optimization
- Sparse signal reconstruction experiments

### Learning-based Photonic Signal Reconstruction

I am also interested in connecting photonic hardware with machine learning models for inverse problems, including sparse signal recovery, decoder-based reconstruction, and algorithmic compensation in integrated photonic systems.
