Zihao Wei

M.Phil Student · Integrated Photonics · Photonic Sensing · AI-assisted Design

I am an M.Phil student at the University of Sydney, supervised by Prof. Xiaoke Yi. My research focuses on computational and integrated photonic systems, including photonic sensing, microwave photonic signal processing, optimization, and AI-assisted photonic design.

Before joining the University of Sydney, I received my B.E. in Computer Science from North University of China and spent time at ICT, Chinese Academy of Sciences.

Email · GitHub · ORCID

Research Focus

Integrated Photonic Sensing

Microring resonators, subwavelength grating structures, resonance shifts, extinction ratio, insertion loss, and sensing-window design.

Microwave Photonic Signal Processing

High-resolution spectrum analysis, microwave photonic filters, filter response design, and reconstruction algorithms.

Optimization for Photonic Systems

Multi-parameter design, simulation-driven search, Bayesian optimization, evolutionary algorithms, and multi-objective evaluation.

AI-assisted Photonic Design

Machine learning for inverse design, sparse signal reconstruction, decoder-based models, and photonic system analysis.

Selected Projects

Localized Subwavelength Grating Ring Resonator for Photonic Sensing

I am developing and analyzing a localized subwavelength grating ring resonator structure for photonic sensing. The project combines Lumerical 3D-FDTD, MODE, INTERCONNECT, and MATLAB/Python post-processing to study resonance shifts, extinction ratio, insertion loss, and sensitivity.

High-resolution Microwave Photonic Filter Design

I have explored multi-channel microwave photonic filter design for high-resolution spectrum analysis, including filter response generation, autocorrelation and cross-correlation analysis, parameter optimization, and signal reconstruction.

AI-assisted Photonic Design and Signal Reconstruction

I am interested in connecting photonic hardware with computational methods, especially for inverse problems, sparse signal recovery, and learning-based compensation in integrated photonic systems.
