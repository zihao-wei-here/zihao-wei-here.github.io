Zihao Wei

M.Phil student at the University of Sydney, supervised by Prof. Xiaoke Yi.

My research interests lie at the intersection of integrated photonics, photonic sensing, microwave photonic signal processing, and AI-assisted photonic design. I am particularly interested in how computational methods, optimization, and machine learning can help design, analyze, and interpret complex photonic systems.

Before joining the University of Sydney, I received my B.E. in Computer Science from North University of China and spent time at ICT, Chinese Academy of Sciences.

Email · GitHub · ORCID

Research Focus

  • Integrated photonic sensing: microring resonators, subwavelength grating structures, and sensing-window design.
  • Microwave photonic signal processing: high-resolution spectrum analysis, filter response design, and reconstruction algorithms.
  • Optimization for photonic systems: parameter search, multi-objective optimization, and simulation-driven design.
  • AI-assisted photonic design: exploring machine learning methods for inverse design, signal reconstruction, and photonic system analysis.

Selected Projects

Localized Subwavelength Grating Ring Resonator for Photonic Sensing

I am developing and analyzing a localized subwavelength grating ring resonator structure for photonic sensing. The project combines 3D-FDTD simulation, MODE analysis, circuit-level INTERCONNECT modeling, and MATLAB/Python post-processing to study resonance shifts, extinction ratio, insertion loss, and sensitivity.

High-resolution Microwave Photonic Filter Design

I have explored multi-channel microwave photonic filter design for high-resolution spectrum analysis. This work involves filter response generation, autocorrelation and cross-correlation analysis, parameter optimization, and signal reconstruction.

AI-assisted Photonic Design and Signal Reconstruction

I am interested in using optimization and machine learning to support photonic design and inverse problems, including sparse signal reconstruction, decoder-based models, and learning-based compensation for photonic systems.
